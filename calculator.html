Mean, Median, and Mode

Mean

The mean is the average of a set of numbers, calculated by dividing the sum of all the numbers by the count of the numbers.

To compute the mean (average) of a set of numbers, follow these steps:

Add Up: Add all the numbers together.

Count : Count how many numbers are in the set.

Divide: Divide the sum by the count of numbers.

Mean = (Σxi) / N

Median

The median is the middle value in a list of numbers, which divides the data into two halves. For an even number of observations, it is the average of the two middle values.

To find the median (middle value) of a set of numbers, do the following:

Sort: Arrange the numbers in ascending order.

Find Middle: If the count of numbers is odd, the median is the middle number. If it's even, the median is the average of the two middle numbers.

Median (Q2) = Middle value (or average of two middle values) when numbers are sorted

Mode

The mode is the number that appears most frequently in a set of numbers.

To determine the mode (most frequent number) in a set of numbers:

Count Occurrences:: Count how many times each number appears in the set.

Find Maximum : Identify the number(s) with the highest frequency. These are the mode(s).

Mode = Most frequently occurring value
